1. Executive Summary & Sizing Methodology

To size the ankle joint actuators for roll and pitch stability, we sweep the joint workspace under two main scenarios: the current robot design (narrow base) and a parallel mechanism (widened base). The sizing maps motor rated and peak limits to ankle output torque capacity using the analytical Jacobian.

For each design, we evaluate standard parallel mounting against decoupled orthogonal mounting. To guarantee safety and performance, the analysis sweeps variations in motor placement depth and linkage attachment offset.

1.1 Kinematics Calculation Method & Formulas

The linkage is modeled as a closed-loop parallel kinematic system. The position of each servo motor's crank tip c_i is constrained by the length of the pushrod L_i to the footplate attachment joint a_i:

Linkage Length Constraint:
||c_i(θ_i) - a_i(p, r)||^2 = L_i^2 (for i = 1, 2)

Where:

  • θ_i: Angular position of Motor i.
  • p, r: Pitch and Roll orientation of the footplate.
  • c_i(θ_i): Position of the crank tip in the global chassis frame.
  • a_i(p, r): Position of the pushrod attachment joint on the footplate in the global chassis frame:
    a_i = a_c + R_x(p) · R_y(r) · a_ib
    where a_c is the central Universal Joint coordinate and a_ib is the attachment joint position in the foot's local frame.

1.2 Jacobian and Torque Mapping

Differentiating the constraint equations with respect to time yields the system Jacobian J, which maps footplate velocities [ṗ, ṙ]ᵀ to motor velocities [θ̇_1, θ̇_2]ᵀ:

Velocity Mapping: θ̇ = J · ẋ
Jacobian Element (Row i, Col j):
J_ij = ((c_i - a_i) · (∂a_i / ∂x_j)) / ((c_i - a_i) · (∂c_i / ∂θ_i))

By the Principle of Virtual Work, the torque transmission is mapped as:

Torque Mapping: τ_ankle = Jᵀ · τ_motor

For a given motor torque limit (Rated/Peak), the ankle output torque is determined as the sum of contributions under motor limits, finding the worst-case minimum capacity across the Pitch (from -25° to +25°) and Roll (from -40° to +40°) sweeps.

5. Sizing Conclusions & Optimization Recommendations

Based on the parametric sweeps across rated/peak outputs, ankle spreads, motor depths, and offsets, we conclude:

  • Effect of Spread (E): Widening the ankle spread from Ankle Spread (E) = 2.3 cm to Ankle Spread (E) = 5.0 cm increases minimum Roll torque by over 2.6x in the Standard Parallel layout (from 44.4 N·m to 119.0 N·m peak on RS03) and increases it by over 1.25x in the Orthogonal layout.
  • Sensitivity of Depth (L): Shifting the motor depth Y-offset (L) outwards (e.g. from 3.6 cm to 4.8 cm for RS03/RS04) significantly improves the Pitch torque capability (minimum Pitch torque increases from 113 N·m to 141 N·m peak on Orthogonal RS03). Recommendation: Keep depth Y-offset (L) as large as allowable by the robot ankle chassis envelope (4.2 cm to 4.8 cm is ideal).
  • Sensitivity of Attach Offset (h): Changing attachment offset (h) to +1.0 cm shifts the peak of kinematic transmission efficiency, leading to higher minimum Pitch torques but lower minimum Roll torques. A nominal h = 0.0 cm is recommended to maintain balanced, symmetric workspace capacities.
  • Final Verdict: The Robstride RS03 is the optimal motor. If a narrow spread E = 2.3 cm must be used, you MUST use the Orthogonal layout to guarantee roll torque above 100 N·m (gives 112.6 N·m peak). If E = 5.0 cm is feasible, either layout with RS03 is robust, but Orthogonal E = 5.0 cm yields the absolute highest minimum roll capacity (145 N·m peak).
