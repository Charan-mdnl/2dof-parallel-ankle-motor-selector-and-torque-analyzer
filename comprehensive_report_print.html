2. Kinematics Calculation Methods & Formulas

The linkage is modeled as a closed-loop parallel kinematic system. The position of each servo motor's crank tip c_i is constrained by the length of the pushrod L_i to the footplate attachment joint a_i:

Linkage Length Constraint:
||c_i(θ_i) - a_i(p, r)||^2 = L_i^2 (for i = 1, 2)

Where:

  • θ_i: Angular position of Motor i.
  • p, r: Pitch and Roll orientation of the footplate.
  • c_i(θ_i): Position of the crank tip in the global chassis frame.
  • a_i(p, r): Position of the pushrod attachment joint on the footplate in the global chassis frame:
    a_i = a_c + R_x(p) · R_y(r) · a_ib
    where a_c is the central Universal Joint coordinate and a_ib is the attachment joint position in the foot's local frame.

2.1 Jacobian and Torque Mapping

Differentiating the constraint equations with respect to time yields the system Jacobian J, which maps footplate velocities [ṗ, ṙ]ᵀ to motor velocities [θ̇_1, θ̇_2]ᵀ:

Velocity Mapping: θ̇ = J · ẋ
Jacobian Element (Row i, Col j):
J_ij = ((c_i - a_i) · (∂a_i / ∂x_j)) / ((c_i - a_i) · (∂c_i / ∂θ_i))

By the Principle of Virtual Work, the torque transmission is mapped as:

Torque Mapping: τ_ankle = Jᵀ · τ_motor

For a given motor torque limit (Rated/Peak), the ankle output torque is determined as the sum of contributions under motor limits, finding the worst-case minimum capacity across the Pitch (from -25° to +25°) and Roll (from -40° to +40°) sweeps.

5. Why Orthogonal? Pitch & Roll Coupling Decoupling

In the Standard Parallel layout, both motor crank shafts are parallel (facing front). When the foot tilts in Roll, the pushrods move in opposite directions (push-pull). However, because the linkages are located in the same vertical plane, they must act against a very narrow lateral spread. This couples the motors and results in highly asymmetric torque: Pitch capacity is high, but Roll capacity is extremely weak.

The Orthogonal layout decouples this system by rotating Motor 1 (the upper actuator) by 90 degrees in yaw, so it sweeps in the YZ plane (perpendicular to Motor 2). This separates the actuators' main planes of action:

  • Motor 1 acts primarily in the pitch plane (sweeping front/back).
  • Motor 2 acts primarily in the roll plane (sweeping left/right).

9.3 Final Mechanical Optimization Recommendations

  • Motor Sizing Verdict: The Robstride RS03 is the optimal actuator selection. It ensures excellent continuous workspace rated torques and provides peak torque limits exceeding 120 N·m for foot strike stabilization.
  • Mounting Choice: If keeping the current chassis footprint (narrow spread E = 2.3 cm), the Orthogonal layout is highly recommended to decouple joint roll and pitch action, preventing critical roll torque collapse (provides 42.71 N·m continuous vs 14.36 N·m in standard layout).
  • Parametric Alignment: Retain the attachment offset at h = 0.0 cm for symmetric workspace limits. Set depth offset to L = 4.2 cm to 4.8 cm to maximize link transmission angles.
